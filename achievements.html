Achievement & Participation Signals



Receiving or Providing Information


Participation is limited to providing information. People are involved in interviews or questionnaire-based documentation of information, beliefs, attitudes, images, or values. No opportunity is genuinely given to influence the process, to contribute to, or even see the final results. The likely outcome for stakeholders is information generation, but that is all.

Achievement & Participation Signals



Consulting or Giving Feedback


Participation involves consultation. People are asked for their views on proposals, which are amended to include those views. This level of engagement may keep participants informed of the results, but there is no real share in the decision-making. A likely outcome for stakeholders is that it generates broader awareness.

Achievement & Participation Signals



Sensemaking & Implementing


Participation becomes functional. Stakeholders help the predetermined objectives of a wider plan or program, but their participation tends to be dependent on external resources and organizations for support and structure. The likely outcome for stakeholders is enabled implementation, as long as support is available.

Achievement & Participation Signals



Identifying & Setting Goals


Participation is interactive through joint analysis and joint action planning. The stakeholders themselves take control, set, and organize a common goal to achieve. A likely outcome for stakeholders is strong sense of shared ownership and long-term implementation structures.

Achievement & Participation Signals



Owning & Maintaining Responsibility


Participation matures through self-governance. Stakeholders take the initiative and self-mobilize. They may contact external organizations for advice and resources, but ultimately stakeholders maintain the control. A likely outcome for stakeholders is very strong sense of ownership and independence.

Achievement & Participation Signals



Influencing Others


Participation and ownerships leads to broader responsibilities for catalyzing change. Community members influence and apply their experiences and best practices with other groups to initiate change. A likely outcome for stakeholders is a strong sense of achievement and peer-recognition.
