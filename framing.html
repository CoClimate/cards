FRAMING



Content Frames


Framing provides coherent reference points, context, and metaphors to help people quickly incorporate new information into their thinking. Content Frames describe WHO, WHAT, WHY, and HOW in order to connect different audiences and their motivations with the issues that matter most to them.

FRAMING



Form Frames


Framing provides coherent reference points, context, and metaphors to help people quickly incorporate new information into their thinking. Form Frames emphasize the WHEN, WHERE, and HOW MUCH, linking temporal, spatial, and emotional motivations to an audience's worldview. Example include loss vs. gain, present vs. future, or local vs. distant.

FRAMING



Metaphors


Metaphors and analogies help translate abstract, unfamiliar, and indirectly experienced concepts into familiar terms that connect with people's emotions. Metaphors connected to taste, touch, and sound, smell, and sight are especially effective; they draw on embodied experiences, making it easier to understand abstract information.

FRAMING



Optimize Default Options


People tend to rely on options that are automatic or preselected. Well-conceived defaults reflect people's characteristics and needs. They are flexible, consistent with overall objectives, and transparent about the options themselves. Continuous monitoring of effectiveness is critical, with periodic updates in response to changing norms, technologies, and situations.

FRAMING



Simplicity


Simplicity minimizes the emotional and cognitive effort it takes to make decisions and solve problems. Factors like time, money, physical effort, brain cycles, social deviance, and non-routine activities tend to impact perceived simplicity. Because people have limited working memory, reduce complicated choices and long lists to between 3 and 5 items.

FRAMING



Social Math


Social math involves putting numbers, statistics, scales, and concepts into a meaningful social context. Start with the data. Develop ideas to make it more relevant. Use a human scale. Compare and contrast numbers with familiar things from everyday life. Refine it until it becomes more intuitive.

FRAMING



Storytelling


The best stories invest their characters with inner emotions, passions, points-of-view, obstacles, and struggles that lead to transformation and understanding. Invoke a sense of wonder. Establish expectations for how events will unfold. Offer just enough detail to keep people anxious and engaged. Let audiences fill in missing information.

FRAMING



Tips & Know-How


Specific action steps can increase people's confidence and clarity around new behaviors and practices. Connect tips and know-how with a well-defined message. Summarize the most important information first. Explain why the recommendations are important. Organize step-by-step directions and simple, short descriptions into chunks with clear headings. 3-5 items is best.
