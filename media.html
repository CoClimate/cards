MEDIA TYPES



Art & New Media


Art signals cultural shifts, new norms, and transformative practices. Art creates psychologically affective experiences. It shifts perception. It reframes how people relate to technology, the environment, and each other. With vast potential for shaping values and practices, art is imagination, and with imagination comes the aptitude for change.

MEDIA TYPES



Games


Games share four defining traits: a goal, rules, a system for feedback, and voluntary participation. Games offer skills practice, safe failure, and experiences of success, social connection, short- and long-term participation, learning, and fun. Games can simulate real-world interactions, role-play different scenarios, and test new experiences.

MEDIA TYPES



Images


Images provide proof, visual evidence, and emotional context. Compose images to show people and personal experiences with objects and familiar surroundings. Clear imagery, iconic images, visual symbols, and visual metaphors can help link abstract information to real world tasks. Avoid charts and graphs, but if unavoidable, visual complexity can help.

MEDIA TYPES



Interface Design


Interface design applies social interactions to tools and built environments. Interfaces communicate social norms, resource efficiency, and the quality of interactions. The design of devices, products, and their interactions can shape attention, expectations, emotions, and interactions through the use of notifications, scales, settings, interruptions, timings, and durations.

MEDIA TYPES



Maps


Maps help people draw connections, make comparisons, and interpret information quickly and effectively. SYSTEMS show connections and relationships between things. PROCESSES depict actions, causation, steps in a path, influence, or cycles. COMPARISONS show shifts, transitions, changes, orders, alignments, and possibilities.

MEDIA TYPES



Toolkits & Instructional Design


Toolkits and instructional design can help make practices more efficient, effective, and appealing through the use of detailed procedures and methods, tips, social proof, demonstrations, and templates. Toolkits organize information and communicate it effectively, leading to well-articulated goals, questions, tasks, approaches, next-steps, and outcomes.

MEDIA TYPES



Videos & Animation


Videos and animation use the expressiveness of moving images to string together sequences of images that are greater than the sum of their parts. They use framing to focus a viewer's attention, sound to enliven emotional reactions, editing to drive a narrative, and images, motion, and graphic relationships to inspire different audience interpretations.

MEDIA TYPES



Visual Communication


Showing, instead of telling, creates insights and breakthroughs. Visuals including infographics, photographs, cartoons, and typography can help resolve ambiguities, fill in gaps, and build a shared mental picture of the actors and factors involved in making change happen.
