Obstacles & Barriers



Doubt & Skepticism


Doubt and skepticism emerge from lack of trust and from reasoning that is motivated and biased by one's identity. Diagnose the source of doubt to respond productively. Find trusted messengers, provide transparency, and develop relevant messages that connect with the things that people care about.

Obstacles & Barriers



Science, Statistics, Scales & Numbers


Translate unfamiliar, abstract, or unintuitive terms and numbers into relatable concepts. Use accessible comparisons, metaphors, or vivid and emotional language.

Obstacles & Barriers



Myths & Misinformation


Avoid reinforcing mistaken beliefs and address myths and misinformation by leading with the correct, core concept you want people to grasp. Use only a few of the clearest, most important ideas to displace myths and outdated concepts. Updating metaphors can help.

Obstacles & Barriers



Uncertainty


People often make decisions under uncertainty. Learning from mistakes is a sign of credibility. Be candid. Focus on what is known. Describe what is uncertain. Provide context, a sense of the scale, and clear visualizations. Group discussions can fill gaps. Identify specific areas of uncertainty, and use them to build plans for action.

Obstacles & Barriers



Security of Belief


Climate change can threaten a person's belief system or worldview. Use the inherent ambiguity of situations to interpret and connect incompatible beliefs. Expressions of symbolism and respect can be valuable for helping people maintain their sense of security and demonstrating that their values are not at risk.

Obstacles & Barriers



Identity Threats


People's self-image and expression of themselves, their priorities, values, and identity can feel under threat from climate change prevention efforts. However, social identities are malleable. People identify with multiple groups and roles. Reframe or invoke alternative identities that connect them with solutions.

Obstacles & Barriers



Emotional Numbing


Emotionally draining and fear-based messages and images reduce people's capacity for concern. Avoid this emotional numbing by linking concrete, personally relevant impacts and experiences to practical, accessible, and embodied practices that individuals and communities can undertake themselves to achieve positive emotional outcomes.

Obstacles & Barriers



Guilt


Overcoming guilt involves setting ambitious but reasonable goals, resolving seemingly irresolvable trade-offs, provisionally re-prioritizing values, or shifting reference frames like the timing of actions. Safe contexts for both failures and successes can build confidence.

Obstacles & Barriers



Helplessness


The causes and consequences of climate change can seem massive, intractable, and overwhelming. To promote a sense of empowerment, reframe the boundaries or scale of their contributions, offer small steps and tiny commitments, make efforts visible, and use initial successes to build towards larger actions.

Obstacles & Barriers



Single Action Bias


Single action bias is the experience people feel when they have "done their part" towards solving a problem. Place simple actions within a larger context, and use first steps to build experience and capacity. Correct misconceptions, and provide a series of steps about which actions are appropriate and which create cumulative impacts.

Obstacles & Barriers



Solutionism


Solutions that are not plausible at the time of communication should not be overly or unrealistically promoted; they foster distrust and unrealistic expectations. Although technological solutions are part of a response to climate change, communicators should highlight the roles and steps that individuals and local communities can play.

Obstacles & Barriers



Structural Barriers


Structural barriers are institutional rules or physical limitations that reduce the options available for change. These involve formal or informal rules and policies, features of technology, or one's natural or built environment. Modify structural barriers to provide leverage for long-term change. This requires sustained effort and skills development to implement.
