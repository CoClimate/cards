SOCIAL



Co-Creation


Co-creation is a process where providers work with consumers to identify, develop, and market solutions that better fit with people's needs, requirements, and identities. Co-creation assumes that users of products and services are better able to provide insights and more informed about their own lives and situations.

SOCIAL



Group Bias


Groups spur inclusion, learning, and diminished selfishness. When physically present or reminded of their group identities, people adjust their goals and individual desires in concert with group goals. Supportive groups provide feedback, communication, and responsiveness, and they focus on local issues that resonate with people's interests and affiliations.

SOCIAL



Personal Experience


Personal stories provide emotional evidence and perspective. Share the significance and why it matters. Describe details such as places, feelings, interactions, or physical movements. Use pauses for emphasis and reflection. Unite the pieces of a story to describe a learned lesson, enhanced understanding, or created an emotional awakening.

SOCIAL



Positive Feedback


Positive feedback improves mood, confidence, and emotional gain despite uncertainty or doubt. It helps individuals and groups evaluate their commitments to new goals in light of their progress. Positive feedback responds to people's identities and needs. It's timely, and it creates experiences of early success that lead to longer-term engagement.

SOCIAL



Public Commitments, Targets & Goals


People follow-through and achieve when they set specific goals for themselves, make a concrete plan, make their goals public, seek support, put something of value on the line, and synergistically bundle their incentives, goals, and aspirations. Appeal to the virtues and aspirations around goals. Emphasize fresh starts rather than failure.

SOCIAL



Rewards & Incentives


Incentives and rewards motivate people, but the type and magnitude matters. Symbolic, non-monetary, and social rewards have the largest impact. They are most effective when offered in public or group settings, and they can intrinsically motivate people to maintain the common good. Material or financial rewards can backfire if they demean closely held values.

SOCIAL



Social Norms


Social norms are implicit rules for how to behave. They vary from group to group, and they are a practical and powerful way to build community. When demonstrated with positive examples from influential group members, social norms reinforce practices, reframe group identity, and reduce misconceptions, outdated information, and false assumptions.

SOCIAL



Social Proof


People are influenced by what others are doing around them. Stories from influential people and groups, positive examples, best practices, ratings, and images can encourage others to follow suit, adopt new behaviors, or modify existing ones. Note: the strength of social proof may vary by culture and worldview.
